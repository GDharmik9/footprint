This document outlines the user roles, sponsor integrations, and user journeys within the **Footprint** ecosystem.

## 🗺️ User Journeys

### 1. Standard User Journey

#### Onboarding
- Complete the 90-second lifestyle archetype form (Housing, diet, transport).
- API builds baseline trajectory and seeds historical logs.
- Initiates the evolving SVG Eco-Sphere sprout and seeds weekly league standings.

#### Habit Tracking & Gamification
- Tracks daily carbon activities (laundry temperature, standby electronics power).
- Completing 7-day streaks awards Leaves (XP).
- Accumulating XP levels up the Eco-Sphere (sprout -> tree -> animated flora).
- Updates weekly standings on the Eco-Leagues leaderboard.

#### Rewards Redemption
- Users spend accumulated Leaves to redeem brand vouchers.
- Vouchers fund real-world impacts (e.g. tree planting via Eden Projects) or sustainable brand discounts.

---

### 2. B-Corp Sponsor Journey

#### Funding Rewards
- Sponsors deposit capital into escrow pools (e.g. $10,000 to sponsor tree-planting campaigns).
- Funds are drawn down as users redeem vouchers (e.g. $1.00 per tree).

#### Campaign Visibility
- Sponsors gain brand placement at moments of user achievements (e.g. Oatly voucher unlocked after plant-based dietary entries).

#### ESG Telemetry
- Sponsors access aggregated ESG metrics (e.g., *"Sponsorship funded 15,000 habits, avoiding 24.3 Metric Tons of CO2"*).
- The platform never shares individual tracking profiles or location data, protecting user privacy.

---

### 3. Platform Admin Journey

- Vets sponsors to verify they are certified B-Corps or meet ESG audit protocols.
- Curates and updates 7-day habit challenges based on seasonal grid constraints.
- Monitors data pipelines, database storage volumes, and third-party API configurations.
